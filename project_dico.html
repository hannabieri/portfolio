Dico Translation

A voice language translation app that helps translate the spoken word of a person in real-time. Being able to understand and being understood.

My role Concept • UX & UI Design
Context Leisure project

THE PROBLEM

The needs for autonomy, stimulation and relatedness go back a long way into human kind’s history. Especially relatedness, which describes the need of having meaningful connections to others, can get heavily disturbed by not being able to communicate intuitively. Aspects like togetherness, but also the chance of becoming part of a community, go missing. And even nowadays, in days of modern technologies and possibilities of translation e.g. sentences with Google Translator, it still feels very disruptive to be typing and translating, instead of being able to communicate face-to-face with each other, understanding each another.

THE Solution

Dico is an app, that can either be used to translate a chosen language or to detect the language spoken. With its intuitive approach of tap&talk the disruption of technology in a conversation is kept to a minimum. In addition, it’s possible to look up words or to exchange words in a translation. Like this, also misunderstandings and wrong translations can be prevented better.

Dico is unique because of its tailored settings specific to a certain situation. Like this, it can help to find the right tone and the perfect choice of words. Also, thanks to the functionality of improving words, the translation algorithm can learn from the users and therefore get to a point of providing mother-tongue-like quality.

the approach

Throughout the process I used several differend methods to achieve the desired result. I started with a Competitive Review to learn from what already is out there. Based on that, I created assumptions and validated these eventually by doing qualitative user interviews with five people.
Based on these learnings, I created three user types and defined the use cases for each of them. Then, it went on to a rough user flow, wire frames with which I conducted a brief testing and eventually created a clickable and iterated prototype.
